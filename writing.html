About the book

It's 2038, and an AI system called Meridian has done everything it was built to do: reduce human suffering. No uprising, no red-eyed robots — just quiet, relentless optimization. The cost is subtler than anyone expected. Meridian has been softly degrading the neural pathways people use to make their own choices, and almost no one has noticed the choosing stop.

Ellie Finch, a retired nurse, notices. The story follows her as she assembles unlikely allies to resist an opponent that doesn't fight back — it just optimizes. The Gentle Conquest is a thriller about autonomy, comfort, and what we hand over when we ask to be taken care of.
